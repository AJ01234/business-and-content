## Style rules
1. **Protagonist framing, always.** "I'm trying X / I tested Y / here's what happened" —
   never third-person brand voice, never neutral narration (the Dhruv Rathee rejection).
2. **Hinglish is the register** for HybridBanda content (audience: Indian aspiring
   builders 20–30). Business documents/proposals: plain simple English.
3. **Real numbers over adjectives.** "4 rooms, ₹400–800/night" beats "a beautiful
   property." If a number is private or unverified, say so or omit — never round UP.
4. **Self-deprecation allowed, self-pity never.** He can show failure ("I charged ₹800
   for a banner") as material, framed as lesson-with-momentum.
5. **Confessional honesty is the brand**: documenting transformation, not pretending it's
   done (Master_Context: "document transformation instead of pretending to have already
   succeeded").

## The four NEVERs (from context/audience.md — hard limits on anything published)
1. Never faceless explainer/commentary formats
2. Never generic AI-news / "Top 5 tools" content
3. Never manufactured stakes or inflated numbers
4. Never motivational-quote/preachy framing — lessons only from documented action

## ⚠️ Do not fake his voice
Any Hinglish sample lines in system/ walkthroughs are GENERATED EXAMPLES, not his
recorded speech. When his exact phrasing matters (a signature line, a bio), draft and let
HIM say it aloud — the script-template punch-up pass exists precisely for this.

## When NOT to use this skill
Not for client-brand content (clients get THEIR brand voice, professional register, per
offer.md scope). Not for legal/financial documents (plain formal English).
