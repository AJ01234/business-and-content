## Content truth rules (from goal.md + audience.md — absolute)
- Never manufacture stakes or inflate numbers. A number said on camera must be one he
  verified (counted, saw in the register, received in UPI).
- Never claim "I quit everything for X" unless factually true.
- If a real number is uncertain, the honest move IS the content: "I don't actually know —
  that's this episode."
- Client/private data: get explicit permission before showing client work or names
  (Dipankar's work needs his OK; wedding footage needs written consent — archive doc).

## Clinic/doctor content compliance gate (before ANY healthcare content ships)
From context/compliance-healthcare.md (research of July 2026; regulations in flux —
re-verify before first clinic client): no patient testimonials/success stories; no
identifiable patient images/scans/before-afters; no cure guarantees or "best in city"
superlatives; no bought followers. Safe formats: doctor-as-educator explainers, facility
tours, FAQs, anonymized education. Every claim in a clinic video gets the doctor's
sign-off — their registration is the thing at risk.

## Proposal/pitch claims
Only claim what track-record.md supports: video creation with repeat clients — yes;
"growth systems that increased X%" — NO until a measured case exists (upsell gate).
Portfolio pieces shown must be his actual work with permission.

## The 60-second pre-ship check (run literally)
1. Every number traceable? 2. Every claim within track record? 3. NEVERs respected?
4. If healthcare: compliance gate passed? 5. Permissions collected? → Ship.

## When NOT to use this skill
Don't let verification become procrastination — his documented failure mode. This gate
takes minutes, not days; "verify" never means "delay outreach to research more."

How to re-verify: walk the registry table top to bottom and update statuses; any row
still ASSUMED after its verify-by action was due is an overdue task, flag it.
