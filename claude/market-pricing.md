# Market Pricing — What India actually pays (researched July 2026)

Web research, July 2026. Use to set and defend prices in offer.md.

## Freelance video editing (per unit)
- Short-form reels: **₹500–₹3,000 per reel** depending on complexity
- Simple projects (reels, short clips, basic YouTube): ₹1,000–₹5,000 per project
- Urgency premiums of 20–50% are normal
- Monthly retainers for consistent deliverables: **₹25,000–₹35,000 per client is a
  commonly cited freelance model**

**Implication:** Atishay's history (₹500–700/video for the real estate client) was
bottom-of-market for basic edits. His floor of ₹15K/month for 8 videos (≈₹1,875/video with
scripting included) sits comfortably inside the normal band — it is NOT expensive. The
₹25–35K Growth package matches the standard retainer norm. Dipankar's ₹5–7K/AI-video is
fair per-unit pricing; hold or raise it.

## Social media management retainers (agency side, India)
- Overall market: ₹5,000 to ₹1,00,000+/month; typical small-business band
  **₹15,000–₹50,000/month**
- Starter packages: ~₹5,000–₹15,000/month for 10–15 posts + basic management
- Growth packages: **₹20,000–₹50,000/month for 20–30 posts** incl. content creation and
  community management
- Premium: ₹60,000–₹1,50,000+/month (40+ posts, influencer work, reporting)
- Startups/small businesses commonly begin at **₹12,000–₹25,000/month**
- Video/reels are priced ABOVE static-post packages everywhere; ad spend is always
  separate from management fees

**Implication:** a clinic or local business paying ₹15–35K/month for a video-led retainer
is a normal, defensible market transaction — not a favor. When a prospect balks, the
objection is trust/value, not price level; answer with proof (samples, case study), never
with a discount below floor.

## Rules derived
1. Never quote below ₹1,500/short-form video equivalent in any bundle
2. Anchor conversations at the Growth package (₹25K) so Starter (₹15K) feels accessible
3. Meta Ads management (RR Garden ask): keep ad SPEND separate from any fee — industry
   standard, and protects him from "the ads didn't work" blame absorbing his fee
4. Revisit this file after the first 10 discovery conversations with real Kanpur numbers
   (Known Gap #3 stays open until then — national averages ≠ Kanpur reality)
