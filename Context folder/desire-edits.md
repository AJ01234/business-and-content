# Desire Edits — Relationship, risk, and how to use it

Confirmed by Atishay, July 2026. (Closes the fact-finding half of Known Gap #4; the
formalization half is still open — see action items.)

## The facts
- **Arrangement:** informal. No fixed pay, no written terms, no employment contract.
- **Compensation:** unpaid-ish — no reliable monthly income from it. Do NOT count it in
  runway math (finances.md correctly assumes ₹0 incoming).
- **Non-compete:** never discussed, nothing written. Taking his own clients — including
  clinic clients — has no stated restriction, but also no explicit clearance.
- **"My team":** Desire Edits' editors, whom he could *probably* hire per-project. Rates
  never agreed. Until rates and availability are confirmed, **the team does not exist for
  planning purposes** — capacity math (delivery-capacity in tools.md / offer.md) assumes
  solo delivery.

## What Desire Edits HAS given him (real assets)
- Cold-calling reps with doctors — the source of the key sales insight: doctors need
  in-person trust to convert
- Exposure to how an editing shop runs client work
- A contact pool of editors (potential contractor bench)

## Risks (rank-ordered)
1. **Time leak without income:** informal work expands to fill weeks while paying nothing.
   It must fit inside the 38–40h budget in goal.md WITHOUT displacing the ~20h
   selling+delivery block — if it can't, it gets cut back.
2. **Relationship ambiguity at the worst moment:** the first time he closes a clinic
   client, an undiscussed "that's our space" conflict could surface. Cheaper to surface it
   now than after a signed retainer.
3. **Phantom capacity:** promising client volumes that assume "the team" before rates are
   agreed repeats the overpromising risk flagged in the archive doc.
