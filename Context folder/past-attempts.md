## HybridBanda, first run
- **Output: 120+ videos published** (long-form + short-form). The lazy narrative "he never
  ships" is FALSE — he shipped a lot.
- **Results: views stuck between ~130 and ~500** despite volume.
- **Why he stopped (his words): exhaustion from low views after posting that much.**
- **Deliberate stance: NO topic niche.** He wants to prove you don't need one; he has
  multiple interests and gets bored locked to one topic. This is a conviction, not
  indecision — respect it.

### Operator's diagnosis (for the second run)
120 videos × no traction is a **packaging/format problem, not a work-ethic problem**. The
fix is NOT a topic niche (he's rejected that and personality-led multi-topic channels do
work — his own references: BeastBoyShub, Casey Neistat). The fix is:
1. **One recognizable protagonist + story format** across all topics (the "one lens" from
   the strategy docs): goal → friction → outcome, him as the doer. Multi-topic is fine;
   multi-IDENTITY is what buried the first run.
2. **Repeatable named series** so viewers know what they're subscribing to emotionally,
   even when topics vary.
3. **Hooks and packaging at the quality-bar.md standard** — the first 3 seconds and the
   title/thumbnail probably killed run #1, not the content quality.
4. **Real stakes this time:** run #1 documented interests; run #2 documents real business
   experiments with money and outcomes — inherently more watchable.
5. **Expectation setting: views are a lagging indicator.** Success metrics for the first
   90 days are published-on-schedule + retention + saves/shares, per audience.md. The
   130–500 view plateau must not be re-read as failure before ~50 posts of the new format.
