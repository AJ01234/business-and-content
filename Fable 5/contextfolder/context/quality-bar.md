# Quality Bar — Whose work defines "good" here

Stated by Atishay, July 2026. Use these as the reference set when judging any HybridBanda
video or client deliverable. "Would this cut hold up next to the reference?" is the review
question.

## The reference set

| Creator | What specifically is the bar |
|---|---|
| **MonkeyMagic** | Storytelling — narrative construction of real-life material |
| **Casey Neistat** | Video editing + storytelling — pace, jump-cut economy, visual wit, story out of an ordinary day |
| **Varun Mayya** | Speaking skill, storytelling, editing — articulate on-camera authority, tight scripting, modern graphics-driven edit |
| **Wrestle Chatter** | Consistency and growth — relentless publishing cadence as the growth engine (proof that consistency itself compounds) |
| **BeastBoyShub** | Cult audience — personality-first content where people follow the PERSON, matching Atishay's stated goal ("recognisable for me and my work, not just watched") |
| **GetSetFly Science (Gaurav Thakur)** | Knowledge depth and documentary delivery — research packaged as gripping long-form |

## What the mix says (synthesis)
The bar is **personality-led, edit-forward storytelling with real substance underneath** —
Hinglish, energetic, cinematic where it earns it. It is NOT polished-corporate, NOT
faceless, NOT topic-first. Long-form ambition = documentary depth (GetSetFly) told with
Neistat/MonkeyMagic narrative energy; short-form = Varun Mayya tightness; operating
discipline = Wrestle Chatter cadence; audience goal = BeastBoyShub-style personal loyalty.

## Deliberately rejected
**Dhruv Rathee.** Whatever the individual reasons (positioning, tone, formula), HybridBanda
does not do: explainer-behind-a-desk political/outrage formatting, thumbnail-driven
alarmism, or content where the presenter is a neutral narrator rather than a protagonist.
Atishay is the protagonist DOING things, not a commentator reporting things.

## Practical review checklist (derived)
Before publishing any HybridBanda video, it must pass:
1. Am I the protagonist doing something real (not explaining someone else's thing)?
2. Is there a story spine — goal, friction, outcome — not just footage?
3. Does the edit move (Neistat/Mayya pace), with no self-indulgent slow sections?
4. Is there at least one moment of genuine substance (number, lesson, insight)?
5. Would I be embarrassed to show this next to the reference set? If yes, fix the ONE
   biggest gap and ship anyway — Wrestle Chatter cadence beats perfection (goal.md: publish
   imperfect evidence).

## Client-work bar
Client deliverables are judged by a different, simpler standard: does it look intentional,
on-brand, and better than what local competitors deliver (see competitors.md), and does it
serve the business outcome? Client work does NOT need to hit the HybridBanda artistic bar —
scope control matters more (2-revision rule in offer.md).
