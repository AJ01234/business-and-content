# Competitors — Who else sells this, and what it proves

Web research, July 2026.

## Kanpur has a real agency market (this matters)
Atishay's belief that "Kanpur doesn't have that kind of market" (operating-rhythm.md) is
contradicted by supply-side evidence: multiple established digital agencies operate IN
Kanpur, several explicitly serving **clinics** and **real estate** — the exact niches in
offer.md. Agencies don't survive in markets where nobody pays. Examples found:

(There are 10+ more in "top agencies in Kanpur" roundups — the long tail is bigger.)

## What the competition is weak at (his opening)
Typical local-agency model: static posts + boosted ads + generic reels, sold by
salespeople, produced by junior editors. Atishay's edges:
1. **Creator-grade video quality** (quality-bar.md standard vs. template reels)
2. **The founder does the work** — the person pitching is the person editing
3. **AI-video capability** with paid proof (Dipankar) — few local shops offer this
4. **Compliance-aware clinic content** (compliance-healthcare.md) — a pitch differentiator
5. **In-person + proof-of-work content** — he can show his own published experiments

## What the competition is strong at (respect it)
- Existing client rosters, case studies, and referral networks
- Sales capacity (they pitch every day; he must match cadence, not talent)
- Bundling ads management — clients often want one vendor for content + ads

## Remote/national competition
For remote clients (the proven Dipankar/Reshma channel), he competes with the national
freelancer pool (₹500–₹3,000/reel — see market-pricing.md). There his differentiators are
reliability, storytelling, and AI-video skill, not price.

## Positioning consequence
Do not pitch as "cheaper agency." Pitch as **the video specialist who personally makes
creator-quality content the big-agency juniors can't** — priced at the market's normal
retainer band (₹15–35K), justified by samples, not promises.
