# Healthcare Marketing Compliance — What clinic content CANNOT do (India)

Web research, July 2026. This is a working summary, not legal advice; verify current
regulations before signing a clinic client, since the rules have been in flux.

## Regulatory state (as researched)
- NMC's **Registered Medical Practitioner (Professional Conduct) Regulations, 2023**
  introduced explicit social-media rules for doctors, but were **put on hold in August
  2024**; the older **2002 MCI ethics code remains in effect**, with NMC panels continuing
  to work on unified norms (including for corporate hospitals).
- Practical stance: design clinic content to satisfy the STRICTER 2023 rules, so nothing
  needs deleting if/when they return.

## What doctor/clinic content must NOT do
1. **No patient testimonials, reviews, endorsements, or "success stories"** as promotion —
   the single biggest difference from normal local-business content
2. **No patient images or identifiable clinical material** — no before/after with
   recognizable patients, no surgical photos, no scans
3. **No misleading, deceptive, or vulnerability-exploiting claims** — no cure guarantees,
   no "best doctor/clinic in Kanpur" superlatives
4. **No bought followers or artificially inflated presence**
5. Announcements should stick to: institution name, types of patients treated, doctor/staff
   qualifications, facilities, and fees

## What clinic content CAN safely be (the service design)
- **Doctor-as-educator:** condition explainers, prevention, "what to expect at a
  consultation," myth-busting — the doctor on camera building trust
- Facility and process tours, staff introductions, equipment explainers
- Anonymized case discussion framed as education (no identifiable patient, no outcome
  promises)
- FAQ formats answering what patients actually search
- This maps perfectly onto Atishay's retainer: scripted educational shorts don't need
  testimonials to work

## Business implications
- **This is a selling point, not a limitation:** most local competitors either ignore
  these rules (risk for the doctor) or don't know them. "I build content that grows your
  practice WITHOUT putting your registration at risk" is a differentiator in the pitch.
- Non-medical adjacent niches (aesthetic salons vs. dermatologists, gyms,
  physio-wellness, coaching, real estate) have far fewer restrictions — pricing and
  content mix can differ accordingly.
- For aesthetic clinics specifically (his warmest clinic contact), be careful:
  aesthetic medicine advertising sits in the greyest zone — get the doctor's consent on
  every claim.
