🔥 PROJECT CONCEPT BRIEF
"NARAKA" — An Adult Animated Musical Series
🌍 THE CORE IDEA
What if Hazbin Hotel was set in the Hindu Underworld — but the chaos wasn't just demonic, it was desi?

Naraka isn't just "hell." In Hindu cosmology it's a temporary purgatory — souls don't stay forever, they get processed and reincarnated. That's your Hazbin Hotel hook but more layered: the stakes aren't "can we redeem sinners to avoid extermination" — the stakes are "the system is broken and nobody gets reincarnated anymore." Naraka is overflowing. The cycle of life and death is jammed.

🏙️ THE WORLD — Narakpur
Think: Neon-lit, overcrowded, mythologically rich underworld city — part Mumbai slum, part ancient temple architecture, part corporate dystopia.

Yamraj runs it like a burnt-out government bureaucrat — too much paperwork, too many souls, completely overwhelmed
Chitragupta (the record-keeper of deeds) is an overworked accountant having an existential crisis because the data doesn't add up anymore
The 28 Narakas from scripture are now different districts of the city — each with its own vibe, gang, culture
Swarga (Heaven) exists above as a gleaming, exclusive, gated society — and they've stopped accepting new entries. No one knows why.
Karma Points are the currency. You earn them, spend them, get scammed with them
The visual aesthetic: dark Madhubani meets neon cyberpunk meets Mughal miniature art — something that looks unmistakably Indian but completely fresh globally.

🎭 THE PROTAGONIST — MUKTI
Full name: Mukti Dharmadhikari
(Mukti = Liberation/Moksha)

Daughter of a minor bureaucrat soul who's been stuck in Narakpur for 300 years
Sharp, sarcastic, deeply empathetic underneath — classic "too smart for this hellhole" energy
She founds "The Moksha Project" — a rehabilitation centre where sinners can genuinely reform and finally get reincarnated
Everyone thinks it's delusional. She doesn't care.
Her flaw: She judges people based on their sins before understanding their story. Her arc is unlearning that.
Why she works globally: Same archetype as Charlie (Hazbin) but grittier, funnier, and her optimism feels earned not naive — she's seen the worst of Narakpur and still chooses hope.

📖 SEASON 1 — THE PLOT ARC
Central Mystery: The reincarnation cycle stopped 7 years ago. Nobody knows why. Narakpur is at breaking point.

Episode Arc (10 episodes):

Chiku arrives, gets lost, stumbles into Mukti's Moksha Project
Mukti tries to get her first "graduate" reincarnated — the system rejects it. No explanation.
Rakt approaches Mukti — he has information about why the cycle stopped, but it costs her
The truth begins emerging: Swarga itself is in crisis — something happened up there, and they've quarantined the entire reincarnation pipeline
Narak-Devi reveals she knows what happened — she was there
Act 2 escalation: Mukti, Agni, Chiku, and Rakt have to infiltrate Swarga
The reveal: Swarga's leadership is corrupt — they've been selecting who gets reincarnated for centuries, favouring certain souls
Yamraj knew and stayed silent. His confrontation with Mukti is the emotional core of the season.
The system doesn't get fixed — but Mukti forces one crack in it. One soul gets through.
Season ends with Narakpur changed but not saved. The fight is real now.
Themes that hit globally:

Systemic corruption vs individual hope
The myth of meritocracy (karma = effort doesn't always = reward)
Found family in the worst possible circumstances
Gen Z exhaustion + Gen Z defiance
